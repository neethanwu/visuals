# Art Deco

## Visual Direction
Art Deco for presentations channels the opulent machine-age glamour of 1920s–30s design: deep black backgrounds saturated with metallic gold, symmetrical compositions, and ornamental geometry borrowed from Egyptian, Aztec, and Modernist sources. Every element earns its place through symmetry and gilded restraint. The double-line frame, the sunburst motif, the greek-key border, and the diamond rule are not decorations — they are the grammar of the style. Text is centered, wide-tracked, and rendered in serif faces with strong decorative weight. The overall mood is ceremonial and authoritative: a deck built in this style signals heritage, craftsmanship, and considered luxury.

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Cinzel Decorative
- **Weights:** 900 (Black)
- **Size Range:** 48–90px
- **Letter Spacing:** 0.25em
- **Style Notes:** All-caps inherent to the typeface; metallic gradient fill (linear-gradient 135deg through gold tones); centered alignment; line-height 1.1

### Body Font
- **Family:** Cormorant Garamond
- **Weights:** 300 (Light), 400 (Regular)
- **Size Range:** 12–15px for slides
- **Line Height:** 2.2

### Label / Sub-heading Font
- **Family:** Cinzel (non-decorative)
- **Weights:** 400, 700
- **Size Range:** 8–16px
- **Letter Spacing:** 0.35–0.6em
- **Style Notes:** All-caps, ultra-wide tracking; used for section numbers, feature labels, and sub-heads

## Design Principles
1. Symmetry is non-negotiable — all primary content is centered and balanced on the vertical axis.
2. Gold is the only accent color; introduce it at varying opacities (10%–100%) to build hierarchy rather than adding new hues.
3. Layered framing devices (double-line border, greek-key band, ornamental rule with diamond glyph) create depth without photography.
4. Decorative motifs — sunburst, ziggurat, lotus — appear at very low opacity (8%–20%) as background texture, never competing with text.
5. Restraint in density: wide margins, generous line-height, and centered text columns no wider than 500–720px keep the composition breathing.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Full black field, centered Cinzel Decorative headline with gold gradient fill, double-line frame at 20–30px inset, ornamental sub-title in wide-tracked Cinzel at 0.35em spacing. Include a sunburst or lotus SVG motif behind the type at ≤12% opacity.
- **Content Slides:** Maintain centered inner column (max 720px). Use a three-column feature grid with thin gold borders (rgba(212,175,55,0.1)). Separate sections with a horizontal gold rule (gradient transparent→gold→transparent) with a small diamond glyph (◆) at center.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Use gold as the primary data color with opacity variants (50%, 30%, 15%) for secondary series. Chart backgrounds should be #0a0a0a; grid lines at rgba(212,175,55,0.1). Avoid colored fills — keep everything in the gold-to-black tonal range.
- **Closing Slides:** Echo the title slide structure. Introduce a full-bleed sunburst at 12% opacity behind closing copy. End on a single ornamental glyph row in wide-tracked Cinzel.
- **Do:** Use wide letter-spacing on all uppercase text; apply the metallic gradient to the single most important word or phrase per slide; keep body copy at light weight (300) to preserve contrast against gold display text.
- **Don't:** Introduce any hue outside the gold-black range (no reds, blues, or greens); use Cinzel Decorative at body sizes; set body copy larger than 15px or it overwhelms the ornamental balance.
