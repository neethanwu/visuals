# Editorial Data

## Visual Direction
Editorial Data is magazine-quality presentation design — the intersection of data journalism and high editorial production. The defining element is Playfair Display italic at dramatic scale: 72px display headlines in italic serif immediately signal that this is not a template deck but an authored, considered piece of communication. The warm off-white canvas (#FDFCF9) reads like paper with photographic quality, and the monochromatic accent strategy — black and dark gray only, no color accent — means all visual hierarchy is earned through typography and scale rather than color cues. Inter handles navigation and body with quiet efficiency. Success and warning states use green and amber but only for genuinely semantic data contexts (growth indicators, caution flags) — never decoratively. This style is ideal for data-rich strategy decks, editorial reporting presentations, media and publishing pitches, and research readouts.

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Playfair Display
- **Weights:** 500 (medium), 700 (bold)
- **Size Range:** 64–80px for title slides (italic at this scale is the signature), 40–52px for section headers, 24–32px for featured data callouts
- **Letter Spacing:** -2px at display sizes (64px+); -1px at 40–52px; 0px below 32px
- **Style Notes:** Italic is the primary register — use Playfair Display italic at 64–80px for every title slide and most section openers. Roman (non-italic) for data callouts and statistical headlines. Bold (700) for hero numbers and single-word emphasis only. The combination of italic serif at dramatic scale and tight negative tracking is the visual fingerprint of this style.

### Body Font
- **Family:** Inter
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium), 600 (semibold)
- **Size Range:** 15–17px body copy, 12–13px navigation, 11px metadata and captions
- **Line Height:** 1.6 for paragraphs, 1.4 for structured lists, 1.2 for navigation items

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Inter (tabular numerals)
- **Weights:** 500 for standard data, 600 for hero KPIs
- **Usage:** All chart axis labels, table values, percentage metrics, and data callouts. Inter with tabular numerals ensures column alignment in data-dense contexts. The monochromatic accent strategy means all data is presented in the #1A1A1A–#999999 grayscale — the absence of color accent makes typography work harder.

## Design Principles
1. Playfair Display italic at dramatic scale is the defining moment of every slide. The 64–80px italic serif headline should feel like a magazine cover line — authored, intentional, editorial. Never substitute with a roman or sans-serif alternative.
2. Monochromatic accent discipline is the advanced constraint. Unlike other editorial styles that permit one accent color, Editorial Data uses only black (#1A1A1A) and its gray scale for all emphasis. Scale, weight, and italic contrast do all the work. Green and amber are reserved exclusively for semantic data states.
3. Negative letter tracking on display type is mandatory. At 64px+, -2px tracking tightens Playfair Display into magazine-headline density. Without it, the type reads as loose and digital rather than editorial and printed.
4. The masthead navigation structure is horizontal. Section navigation across the top of content slides uses Inter 600, small caps or uppercase, +0.5px tracking, separated by hairline vertical rules or dot separators in #E5E2DC.
5. The logo/brand identifier uses +4px letter spacing — the widest tracking value in the system. This creates maximum breathing room around brand elements, signaling premium positioning.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Warm off-white (#FDFCF9) background. Title in Playfair Display 500 italic at 72–80px, #1A1A1A, flush-left. The -2px tracking compresses the italic text into a dense editorial block. Issue/deck identifier in Inter 600 uppercase, 10px, +4px tracking, #777777, positioned as a logo lockup in the top-left. Date and subtitle in Inter 400, 16px, #777777.
- **Content Slides:** Off-white background. Horizontal masthead rule: Inter 600 uppercase section labels, 10px, +0.5px tracking, #777777, separated by hairline vertical rules. A full-width 1px border (#E5E2DC) beneath the masthead. Body in Inter 400, 16px. Featured pull quote in Playfair Display 500 italic, 24–28px, with a 1px left border in #1A1A1A.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Chart title in Playfair Display 700 (roman), 24–32px, -1px tracking. Axis labels in Inter 500, 11px, #777777. Data values in Inter 600, 13px, #1A1A1A. Gridlines as 1px #E5E2DC horizontal rules. Growth indicators use #22C55E sparingly; risk/caution in #F59E0B. No colored data series — use position, label, and value annotation for distinction.
- **Closing Slides:** Off-white background. Playfair Display 500 italic closing statement at 64–72px, flush-left. Inter 400, 15px, #777777 for contact and follow-up details. Deck identifier in Inter 600 uppercase, 10px, +4px tracking, #777777, top-left as a consistent brand mark.
- **Do:** Use Playfair Display italic at 64px+ on every title. Apply -2px tracking at display sizes. Maintain the monochromatic constraint — resist adding color accents.
- **Don't:** Use Playfair roman at display sizes on title slides — italic is the register. Apply green or amber decoratively. Add rounded corners, shadows, or gradients. Use colored data series in charts.
