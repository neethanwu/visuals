# Bauhaus Digital

## Visual Direction
Bauhaus Digital translates the Dessau constructivist manifesto into contemporary slide design. The palette is the Bauhaus primary triad — red, blue, yellow — deployed as hard geometric shape markers against near-white backgrounds with heavy 2px black borders anchoring every structural element. Space Grotesk drives headings with aggressive negative tracking at display sizes, while Space Mono lends data labels and metrics a technical, systematic character. There is no ornamentation that does not serve a structural purpose: shapes are navigation markers, borders are load-bearing, colors are categorical. This style is authoritative in product strategy decks, design system presentations, engineering roadmaps, and anywhere the audience respects systematic thinking over decorative polish.

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Background | #FAFAFA | Warm off-white — softer than pure white, recedes behind black borders |
| Text Primary | #000000 | Pure black — maximum contrast, uncompromising |
| Accent Red | #E53935 | Bauhaus red — danger, emphasis, primary CTA |
| Accent Blue | #1E88E5 | Bauhaus blue — information, links, secondary data |

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Space Grotesk
- **Weights:** 600 (semibold), 700 (bold)
- **Size Range:** 56–80px for title slides, 32–48px for section headers
- **Letter Spacing:** -2px at display sizes (56px+); -1px at section header sizes
- **Style Notes:** Uppercase is permitted for short headers (4 words or fewer) at section level. Never italic. Bold weight preferred for primary titles.

### Body Font
- **Family:** Space Grotesk
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium)
- **Size Range:** 15–18px body, 13px supporting text
- **Line Height:** 1.5 body, 1.3 lists

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Space Mono
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium — use bold for emphasis in mono)
- **Usage:** All data labels, percentage callouts, metric badges, table headers, code snippets, and any technical annotation. Set at +1px letter spacing to aid legibility at small sizes.

## Design Principles
1. The three primary colors are categorical, not decorative. Assign one color to one data series, section, or concept and maintain that assignment without deviation throughout the deck.
2. Heavy 2px black borders define all structural containers. Cards, tables, stat blocks, and callout boxes all use visible black outlines — no hairlines, no ghost borders.
3. Geometric shapes (circle, square, triangle) function as bullet and category markers. Use them consistently as section identifiers rather than arbitrary icons.
4. Zero corner radius everywhere. Sharp 90-degree corners are the geometric signature of this style — rounding anything contradicts the aesthetic.
5. Space Mono labels give systematic authority to data. Wherever a number, percentage, or technical term appears in a callout or badge, it belongs in mono — never in the display font.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Black (#000000) background is permitted for maximum impact. Title in Space Grotesk 700 at 72–80px, white text. A colored geometric shape (circle for chapter 1, square for chapter 2, triangle for chapter 3) placed in the upper-left corner as a section marker. Subtitle in Space Mono 400, 14px, +1px tracking.
- **Content Slides:** White (#FAFAFA) background. All content containers outlined with 2px black borders. Section category label in Space Mono uppercase, 11px, +1px tracking, colored with the assigned category accent. Body in Space Grotesk 400, 16px.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Use the primary color triad for up to three data series. Chart container has a 2px black border. Axis labels in Space Mono 400, 12px. Data callout badges in Space Mono 500 with a colored background block and white or black text for contrast.
- **Closing Slides:** Return to black background. Single large statement in Space Grotesk 700. All three accent colors used in a row of three geometric markers beneath the text to signal completeness. Contact in Space Mono 400, 14px.
- **Do:** Use borders on all containers. Assign colors categorically and stick to them. Use Space Mono for every data label and metric.
- **Don't:** Round any corners. Use gradients or shadows. Mix the three accent colors randomly without categorical intent. Use more than three accent colors in a single slide.
