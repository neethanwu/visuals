# Neo-Expressionism

## Visual Direction
Neo-Expressionism brings the raw emotional charge of Basquiat, Kiefer, and Schnabel into the presentation format. The background is warm aged paper (#f2ead8) — a ground that reads as found rather than designed — onto which gestural paint splatters, drip marks, and irregular blobs are laid at low opacity, simulating the accumulated surface of a working canvas. Headlines are set in Permanent Marker, a handwriting face that communicates urgency and authorship, slightly rotated at –1.5deg to break the grid's authority. Caveat handles sub-headings with loose, weighted script energy. The color vocabulary is raw: cadmium red-orange, cobalt blue, raw sienna — all desaturated and translucent, applied as texture rather than fill. The overall register is charged, personal, and anti-corporate — a deck that looks like it was made by someone who means it.

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Permanent Marker
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular — single weight typeface)
- **Size Range:** 52–110px
- **Letter Spacing:** Default
- **Style Notes:** Rotated –1.5deg to break orthogonal alignment; line-height 0.95 for dense stacking; color #2a1a10; also used for section numbers (13px, rgba(180,40,20,0.5)) and characteristic list items (15px, #3a2818)

### Body Font
- **Family:** DM Sans
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular)
- **Size Range:** 14px for slides
- **Line Height:** 2.0

### Sub-heading Font
- **Family:** Caveat
- **Weights:** 700 (Bold)
- **Size Range:** 22–36px
- **Letter Spacing:** Default
- **Style Notes:** Rotated +0.5deg; color rgba(180,40,20,0.6); used for the emotional register line beneath the headline; handwritten quality contrasts with the harsher Permanent Marker above

## Design Principles
1. The painted surface comes first — splatters and drips establish that this is a canvas, and all typographic decisions should honor that physicality.
2. Rotation is deliberate disruption: the headline at –1.5deg and sub-heading at +0.5deg signal that precision is being consciously rejected in favor of gesture.
3. The paint marks (four splatters, two drips) should be positioned asymmetrically — one cluster in the upper-left, one in the lower-right, with single marks crossing the center — to create the appearance of actual paint behavior.
4. Hierarchy is handled by typeface contrast alone: Permanent Marker for authority, Caveat for emotional annotation, DM Sans for informational content — never mix their roles.
5. The em-dash prefix (— ) on characteristic list items functions as a mark or stroke, not a bullet — it carries the gesture of the whole style in miniature.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Full-bleed #f2ead8 background with all four splatter shapes and two drip marks positioned. Permanent Marker headline at 80–110px, –1.5deg rotation, #2a1a10. Caveat sub-heading at 28–36px, weight 700, rgba(180,40,20,0.6), +0.5deg rotation, below the headline with 48px margin. Section number in 13px Permanent Marker, rgba(180,40,20,0.5), above headline.
- **Content Slides:** Asymmetric two-column grid (1.3fr / 1fr, 60px gap). Left column: DM Sans body at 14px, line-height 2.0, color #6a5848. Right column: characteristic list using Permanent Marker at 15px, #3a2818, each item preceded by "— " in rgba(180,40,20,0.5), separated by 2px border-bottom rules in rgba(180,40,20,0.12). One or two subtle splatter shapes positioned in the corners.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Use the warm earth palette for chart fills (#6a5848, rgba(180,40,20,0.5), rgba(26,51,102,0.5)). Avoid clean geometric chart styles — prefer loose point plots or hand-drawn style charts. Data labels in DM Sans, body copy color. Add a single Caveat annotation at 0.5deg rotation to the most important data point.
- **Closing Slides:** Single Permanent Marker phrase at maximum size, full rotation treatment. A Caveat line beneath it in the red-orange accent. Apply notes in DM Sans at 11px, text-transform uppercase, letter-spacing 0.12em, color #aaa — the only moment of restraint, providing closure contrast.
- **Do:** Let the paint splatters overlap the edges of the canvas; use rotation on every headline, never set Permanent Marker text at 0deg; allow the background to show through and around all typographic elements.
- **Don't:** Clean up the composition into a grid-aligned layout; use any sans-serif other than DM Sans for body copy; add photography or polished illustration — the raw drawn quality is everything.
