# Swiss Clean

## Visual Direction
Swiss Clean channels the Zurich school of graphic design — rigorous grid alignment, unambiguous hierarchy, and a single vivid red accent that cuts through like a signal flare. Every layout decision is earned through function: no decorative borders, no drop shadows, no gradient fills, no rounded corners to soften the geometry. Space Grotesk brings geometric precision to headings with tight negative letter spacing on display sizes, while Inter handles body copy with quiet authority. The palette is surgical — off-black on white with one red that means business. This style is ideal for enterprise strategy decks, financial presentations, and any context where credibility must be established in the first two seconds of a slide.

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Background | #FFFFFF | Pure white — maximum contrast foundation |
| Text Primary | #0D0D0D | Near-black, warmer than pure black |
| Text Secondary | #7A7A7A | Mid-gray for supporting copy and captions |
| Accent | #E42313 | Vivid Swiss red — use sparingly, one element per slide |
| Border | #E8E8E8 | Hairline dividers and structural rules |

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Space Grotesk
- **Weights:** 500 (medium), 600 (semibold)
- **Size Range:** 48–72px for title slides, 32–40px for section headers
- **Letter Spacing:** -1px on all display sizes; 0px at body sizes
- **Style Notes:** Never italic. All display text set flush-left. Avoid all-caps except for short category labels (8–10px, +2px tracking).

### Body Font
- **Family:** Inter
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium)
- **Size Range:** 14–18px body copy, 12px captions and footnotes
- **Line Height:** 1.5–1.6 for paragraphs, 1.2 for tight lists

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Inter (tabular numerals via font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums)
- **Weights:** 500
- **Usage:** All numeric data in tables and charts; ensures column alignment

## Design Principles
1. One accent color per slide, maximum. Red is a signal, not a decoration — reserve it for the single most important element: a key number, a CTA, a critical label.
2. Grid alignment is non-negotiable. All elements snap to a consistent column grid. Misalignment reads as incompetence in this style.
3. Whitespace is structural, not empty. Generous margins and gutters are part of the design — do not fill them.
4. No shadows, no gradients, no rounded corners. Any softening of geometry undermines the Swiss precision ethos.
5. Hierarchy through scale and weight only. Size and weight differences do the entire organizational job — color and decoration are not allowed to compensate.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Full-bleed white background. Title in Space Grotesk 600 at 64–72px, flush-left, near the vertical center or upper third. A single 2px red horizontal rule beneath the title. Subtitle in Inter 400, 18px, #7A7A7A.
- **Content Slides:** Two-column or three-column grid with 40px gutters. Section label in Space Grotesk 500 uppercase at 10px, #7A7A7A, +2px tracking above the content block. Body in Inter 400, 16px.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Chart title in Space Grotesk 600, 24px. Use red (#E42313) exclusively for the primary data series. All other series in #0D0D0D and #7A7A7A. No chart borders — use axis rules in #E8E8E8 only.
- **Closing Slides:** Mirror the title slide. A single large Space Grotesk 600 statement at 56px. Red accent on one word or phrase only. Contact/URL in Inter 400, 14px, #7A7A7A bottom-left.
- **Do:** Use the red accent to direct the eye to exactly one thing per slide. Let whitespace breathe. Maintain flush-left alignment throughout.
- **Don't:** Use gradients, box shadows, rounded corners, or more than one accent color per slide. Never center-align body copy. Avoid decorative icons.
