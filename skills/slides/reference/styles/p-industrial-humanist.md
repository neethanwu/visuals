# Industrial Humanist

## Visual Direction
A disciplined collision of two opposing traditions: condensed industrial typography (Barlow Semi Condensed, uppercase, wide-tracked) announces each section with factory-floor efficiency, while Source Serif 4 carries the body text with warm editorial humanity. The canvas is warm stone (#F4F3F1), not sterile white — evoking uncoated paper or concrete aggregate. White cards float on this canvas with subtle drop shadows, creating a tactile, physical layering. The palette is restrained and earthy: forest green (#3D7A5A) marks completion and positive outcomes; burnt orange (#C45C26) signals progress and energy. Zero corner radius everywhere — sharp edges reinforce the industrial character. Section labels are small, uppercase, and breathe with +3px letter-spacing, acting as structural signposts throughout the slide.

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Background | #F4F3F1 | Warm stone — the primary canvas, uncoated-paper quality |
| Card Surface | #FFFFFF | Pure white cards for content containers |
| Text Primary | #1C1C1C | Near-black; rich but not harsh against warm stone |
| Text Secondary | #5C5C5C | Mid-gray for supporting copy, captions, metadata |

| Accent Orange | #C45C26 | Burnt orange — progress indicators, energy, active states |

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Barlow Semi Condensed
- **Weights:** 600 (section labels), 700 (high-emphasis labels, category markers)
- **Size Range:** 11–14px for section labels (ALL-CAPS), 18–24px for card labels and sublabels
- **Letter Spacing:** +3px on ALL-CAPS section labels; standard tracking on larger sizes
- **Style Notes:** Used exclusively for labels, section headers, and categorical markers — never for body copy or long text. The condensed width and uppercase treatment create an efficient, information-dense label system that doesn't compete with the serif headlines.

### Body Font
- **Family:** Source Serif 4
- **Weights:** 400 (body copy, descriptions), 600 (slide titles, section headings, key callouts)
- **Size Range:** 36–52px for slide titles (600), 18–24px for section headings (600), 14–16px for body (400)
- **Line Height:** 1.5 for body paragraphs, 1.2 for large headline stacks

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Source Serif 4
- **Weights:** 600
- **Usage:** Large metric figures and KPIs. Set at -1px letter-spacing for dense, weighty numerals. The serif character gives data a credible, editorial feel rather than a tech-startup lightness.

## Design Principles
1. The label system governs the hierarchy — Barlow Semi Condensed ALL-CAPS labels (+3px) appear before every content zone, acting as a consistent grid of signposts.
2. Serif warmth earns trust — Source Serif 4 in the headline and body position signals credibility, depth, and editorial authority, not digital sterility.
3. Stone canvas over white — the #F4F3F1 background distinguishes this system from default corporate decks; white is reserved for card surfaces only.
4. Two accents, two meanings — forest green is always positive/complete; burnt orange is always progress/active. Never swap or mix their semantic roles.
5. Shadow creates materiality — white cards on stone background use a subtle drop shadow (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)) to suggest physical layering, like papers on a desk.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Stone background, large Source Serif 4 600 title (52px), subtitle in 400 at 20px. A Barlow Semi Condensed ALL-CAPS label in burnt orange at the top-left marks the presentation category or client name.
- **Content Slides:** Stone canvas, white cards with subtle shadow. Barlow label at top of each card. Body text in Source Serif 4 400. Use forest green and burnt orange only for status indicators and metric highlights.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Source Serif 4 600 for metric figures (-1px tracking). Barlow Semi Condensed ALL-CAPS axis labels and chart section labels (+3px). Forest green for positive trends, burnt orange for growth/progress series.
- **Closing Slides:** Large Source Serif 4 600 closing statement on stone. Barlow ALL-CAPS label marks the slide type. Single burnt orange accent element (underline or block) for energy.
- **Do:** Use Barlow Semi Condensed labels to introduce every content zone, even in simple slides. Let the stone background show — don't cover it with cards edge-to-edge.
- **Don't:** Set body copy in Barlow — it's a label font only. Apply corner radius to any element. Mix the semantic meanings of the two accent colors.
