# Nordic Brutalist

## Visual Direction
A warm brutalist aesthetic built on tension between a near-black charcoal sidebar and a cream content field. The palette is earthy and grounded — terracotta (#C05A3C) serves as the singular accent against cream and taupe, evoking Scandinavian craft traditions filtered through brutalist discipline. All typography is set in Space Grotesk for structural headings and Inter for readable body text. Labels are always uppercase with +1px letter-spacing, creating a systematic, almost typographic grid across slides. Borders carry weight (2–4px) and are structural rather than decorative. Corner radius is zero throughout — every card, tag, and container is square-cornered without exception. Dark inversions (charcoal cards, reversed text) are used strategically to signal emphasis sections, hero metrics, or primary calls to action.

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Space Grotesk
- **Weights:** 600 (section titles), 700 (slide titles, key metrics)
- **Size Range:** 36–56px for slide titles, 20–28px for section headings, 14–16px for labels
- **Letter Spacing:** +1px on uppercase labels; standard tracking on titles
- **Style Notes:** ALL-CAPS treatment mandatory for section labels, tags, and category markers. Use 700 weight for hero numbers and primary titles. Pairs with square card shapes and heavy borders for a unified constructivist feel.

### Body Font
- **Family:** Inter
- **Weights:** 400 (body text, descriptions)
- **Size Range:** 13–16px for body copy, 11–12px for footnotes/captions
- **Line Height:** 1.6 for body paragraphs, 1.3 for tight label stacks

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Space Grotesk
- **Weights:** 600–700
- **Usage:** All numerical data, KPIs, chart labels, and percentage figures. The geometric character of Space Grotesk keeps numbers feeling structural rather than editorial.

## Design Principles
1. Square everything — zero corner radius is non-negotiable. Any rounding would undermine the brutalist integrity of the system.
2. Borders are structure — use 2px borders as dividers between content zones, and 4px borders to frame emphasis cards or key callouts.
3. Labels are uppercase always — every section title, tag, badge, and category marker is set in ALL-CAPS Space Grotesk with +1px letter-spacing.
4. Dark inversions signal importance — flip card backgrounds to #1A1A1A with #F5F3EF text for hero metrics, primary stats, or closing calls to action.
5. Terracotta is singular — use #C05A3C for one focal accent per slide (an underline, a bullet marker, a key figure) to preserve its visual weight.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Dark inversion — full #1A1A1A background, Space Grotesk 700 title in #F5F3EF, terracotta accent underline or block beneath subtitle. Logo area uses +2px letter-spacing.
- **Content Slides:** Cream (#F5F3EF) background, taupe (#E8E4DC) cards, 2px #1A1A1A borders on all card containers. Section label in ALL-CAPS 14px Space Grotesk 600 with +1px spacing at top-left.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Use terracotta for primary data series, forest green for positive indicators, muted teal for secondary series. Chart labels in Space Grotesk 600, axis labels in Inter 400.
- **Closing Slides:** Return to dark inversion. Large Space Grotesk 700 statement text. Terracotta used for a single CTA element or divider line.
- **Do:** Use heavy 4px borders to frame the single most important element on any given slide. Use ALL-CAPS labels to create a consistent typographic grid.
- **Don't:** Round any corners. Use more than one accent color per slide. Apply terracotta to decorative elements — it must always mark something meaningful.
