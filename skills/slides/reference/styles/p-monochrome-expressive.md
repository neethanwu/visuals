# Monochrome Expressive

## Visual Direction
Monochrome Expressive is the most disciplined system in this collection — zero accent colors, zero hue of any kind. All emphasis is achieved exclusively through the contrast between pure black (#0A0A0A) and pure white (#FFFFFF), modulated by typographic weight and scale. The structural signature is banded layout: a black header band, a white content body, and a black footer band, creating a visual cadence that reads with newspaper authority across every slide in a deck. DM Serif Display at 92px is the gravitational center of the system — its dramatic scale in a pure black-and-white field creates an expressiveness that no accent color could improve. Inter handles all navigation and body with clean hierarchy. Dot separators in the navigation reinforce the print typographic tradition. This style communicates timeless authority and is ideal for thought leadership keynotes, institutional annual reports, legacy brand presentations, and premium consulting deliverables.

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Background | #FFFFFF | Pure white — the content field |
| Header / Footer | #0A0A0A | Near-black for banded structural elements |
| Text Primary | #0A0A0A | Near-black for all body and headline text on white |
| Text Secondary | #999999 | Mid-gray for supporting content and captions |
| Border | #E0E0E0 | Light gray hairline dividers within content zones |
| Surface | #F5F5F5 | Light gray for subtle content cards and inset panels |

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** DM Serif Display
- **Weights:** 400 (regular) — DM Serif Display has one weight; expressiveness comes from scale
- **Size Range:** 80–92px for title slides (the system's defining scale), 48–64px for section headers, 36–44px for slide subtitles
- **Letter Spacing:** -3px at page title scale (80–92px) — the tightest tracking in this collection; -2px at section header scale (48–64px); 0px below 36px
- **Style Notes:** DM Serif Display should always appear in black (#0A0A0A) on white backgrounds or in white on the black bands. Never gray. Never at small sizes — it is a display-only typeface. The -3px tracking at 92px creates an intense, compressed headline mass that is the visual identity of this system.

### Body Font
- **Family:** Inter
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium)
- **Size Range:** 15–18px body copy, 12–13px navigation and supporting text, 11px metadata
- **Line Height:** 1.5 for paragraphs, 1.3 for lists, 1.2 for navigation

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** DM Serif Display (for metric callouts) + Inter (for data labels)
- **Weights:** 400 (DM Serif for hero metrics), 500 Inter for axis labels and table data
- **Usage:** Hero KPI numbers use DM Serif Display at 64–80px with -2px tracking — the typographic weight of the serif makes numbers feel monumental. Axis labels and table values use Inter 500 with tabular numerals. All in black and white — no colored data series.

## Design Principles
1. Zero accent colors is the supreme constraint. The monochrome discipline is not a limitation — it is the system's expressive power. Every attempt to introduce a color accent weakens the authority of the black-and-white contrast. The answer is always more scale, more weight, more whitespace — never a color.
2. The banded structure (black header, white body, black footer) is a mandatory layout framework. Every slide in a deck built in this style should maintain this three-band architecture, creating visual consistency and a print-publication rhythm across the deck.
3. DM Serif Display at 80–92px with -3px tracking is the system's most powerful tool. At this scale and tracking, the letterforms compress into a near-monolithic typographic mass. This is where the style earns the word "expressive" — do not compromise on scale.
4. Logo and brand identifiers use +2px tracking in Inter — the widest positive tracking value in the system. This creates deliberate breathing room in the black header band around brand text, signaling premium positioning through restraint.
5. Dot separators are the navigation punctuation. In header navigation and footer content, Inter navigation items are separated by small dot characters (·) in #999999. This is a print typographic convention that reinforces the timeless, authoritative register.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Black (#0A0A0A) header band (15–20% of slide height) containing the brand/deck identifier in Inter 500 uppercase, 11px, +2px tracking, white. White content body. Title in DM Serif Display 400 at 80–92px, #0A0A0A, flush-left, -3px tracking — the title should feel monumental and compressed. Subtitle in Inter 400, 18px, #999999. Black footer band (8–10% of slide height) containing presenter/date in Inter 400, 11px, white.
- **Content Slides:** Black header band with section identifier in Inter 500 uppercase, 11px, +2px tracking, white, dot-separated from the deck title. White content body. Section header in DM Serif Display 400 at 48–56px, -2px tracking. Body in Inter 400, 16px, #0A0A0A. Inset content panels use #F5F5F5 surface with a 1px #E0E0E0 border. Black footer with slide number in Inter 400, 11px, white.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Black header band with chart section label. White body. Hero KPI in DM Serif Display 400 at 64–80px, -2px tracking, #0A0A0A. Supporting metrics in Inter 500, 32px. Chart with black bars/lines on white, gridlines in #E0E0E0. Axis labels in Inter 500, 11px, #999999. All in pure monochrome — no colored series. Black footer.
- **Closing Slides:** Full black header band (expanded to 25% for visual impact). White body with DM Serif Display 400 closing statement at 72–80px, -3px tracking. Inter 400 contact line, 14px, #999999. Black footer band with brand lockup in Inter 500 uppercase, +2px tracking, white.
- **Do:** Maintain the three-band structure on every slide. Apply -3px tracking at 80–92px display sizes. Use dot separators in navigation. Let the scale do the expressive work that color cannot.
- **Don't:** Introduce any accent color — not even a single decorative element in a non-black/white hue. Reduce the display type below 48px on headers. Round any corners. Add shadows or gradients.
