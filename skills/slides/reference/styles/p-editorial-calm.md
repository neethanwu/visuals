# Editorial Calm

## Visual Direction
Editorial Calm is a deliberate rejection of the visual language of SaaS and tech presentations. There are no images, no icons, no shadows, no gradients, no decorative elements of any kind. Typography is the entire design system. Newsreader brings a literary, long-form reading quality to headlines — it is a typeface that belongs in The Atlantic or The New Yorker, not a startup pitch deck, and that incongruity is exactly the point. The palette is green-gray monochrome: forest green (#1E3322) for primary text, olive-sage (#6B7B6B) for secondary, with a deliberately varied dot palette for category markers. CTAs are inline text treatments, never button shapes. Whitespace is not empty — it is intentional structure. This style is ideal for thought leadership decks, consulting strategy presentations, nonprofit annual reports, and any context where calm authority matters more than visual excitement.

## Typography

### Display Font
- **Family:** Newsreader
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium)
- **Size Range:** 52–68px for title slides, 32–44px for section headers, 24–28px for featured pull quotes
- **Letter Spacing:** 0px default; never apply negative tracking to Newsreader — its letterforms are designed for generous spacing
- **Style Notes:** Italic is the editorial register — use Newsreader italic for pull quotes, literary statements, and the single most significant phrase on a closing slide. Regular for titles and section headers. Never bold (600+).

### Body Font
- **Family:** Inter
- **Weights:** 400 (regular), 500 (medium), 600 (semibold)
- **Size Range:** 15–18px body copy, 12–13px navigation and supporting text, 11px metadata
- **Line Height:** 1.7 for paragraphs (generous — print editorial standard), 1.4 for lists, 1.2 for navigation labels

### Data/Mono Font (if applicable)
- **Family:** Inter (with tabular numerals)
- **Weights:** 500
- **Usage:** Numeric data in tables and metrics. The monochromatic system means all data is presented in the green-gray palette — no colored data series. Structure comes from column alignment, not color coding.

## Design Principles
1. Typography is the only structural tool. There are no icons, images, decorative shapes, or visual elements beyond type, rules, and dot markers. Every layout decision must be solved through typographic means.
2. Whitespace is structural, not empty. Margins of 80–120px on title slides and 60–80px on content slides are not padding — they are the breathing room that gives Newsreader headlines the space to land. Resist the impulse to fill.
3. CTAs are inline text links, never button shapes. The anti-SaaS ethos means all calls to action are typographically integrated — bold weight, forest green, perhaps underlined, but never enclosed in a shape.
4. The dot palette is categorical, not decorative. The five dot colors are used as small (6–8px) circular category markers in navigation and lists. They are the only multi-color element in the system.
5. Uppercase labels with +1.5px tracking are the navigation register. All nav items, section headers, and table column labels use Inter 500 or 600 uppercase with +1.5px tracking. This is the system's only typographic capitalization rule.

## Slide Application Notes
- **Title Slides:** Pure white background. Title in Newsreader 400 at 56–68px, forest green (#1E3322), flush-left, generous top margin (30% from top). A single-word or short subtitle in Inter 400, 18px, #6B7B6B. Presenter/date in Inter 400, 11px, #6B7B6B, bottom-left. No rules, no lines, no shapes.
- **Content Slides:** Pure white. Section label in Inter 600 uppercase, 10–11px, #6B7B6B, +1.5px tracking, top-left. Body in Inter 400, 16–17px, #1E3322, line-height 1.7. A small 6px dot in the appropriate category color precedes each list item in categorized lists.
- **Data/Chart Slides:** Forest green (#1E3322) as the sole data color. Grid lines in a very light tint of #6B7B6B at 20% opacity. Column headers in Inter 600 uppercase, 10px, +1.5px tracking. All values in Inter 500, 14px. No colored series — use position and label to distinguish data categories.
- **Closing Slides:** White background, vast whitespace. Newsreader 400 italic at 44–56px, forest green, centered or flush-left. A single inline CTA in Inter 600, 16px, #2D6B3F with no button enclosure. Contact details in Inter 400, 12px, #6B7B6B.
- **Do:** Embrace vast whitespace. Use Newsreader italic for the one moment that needs literary weight. Apply dot markers categorically and consistently.
- **Don't:** Add icons, images, shadows, gradients, or button shapes. Use negative letter spacing. Apply the terracotta or amber dots unless you have a defined category for them.
